OUR TEAMS

Collaborative by nature

Every team contributes to our films. Working closely together, our Production, Technology, and Studio Operations teams create stories that define eras.

Production

Our Production departments are the beating heart of our creative, orchestrating the seamless coordination and execution of every film with utmost precision and dedication. From the initial spark of an idea to the final frame, we work with animators, bringing them to life with breathtaking richness and expression.

Our technical directors ensure that every detail is perfect, from lighting and textures to effects and animation. And our production management teams bring everything together seamlessly, ensuring that our projects are delivered on time and on budget.

Technology

Revolutionary technology is in our DNA. Our tech teams are constantly pushing the boundaries of what's possible in the realm of animation, with their expertise and passion for developing cutting-edge tools and techniques, they breathe life into our characters and worlds, delivering a truly immersive experience to our audiences.

Studio Operations

Our Studio Operations departments are the backbone of our studio, supporting the creative process and ensuring the behind-the-scenes infrastructure that keep everything running smoothly. Their dedication, compassion, and immaculate expertise have an invaluable impact on the heart of our films. From their essential functions, like Marketing, Finance, Legal, and People, among many others, our creative wouldn't be possible, and they truly make the company happen extraordinarily.

FAQ's

We recommend submitting your resume and cover letter in PDF format. PDFs maintain formatting consistency across different devices and operating systems. Make sure your files are clearly named, such as "FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf" and "FirstName_LastName_CoverLetter.pdf" for easy identification by our recruiting team.

You can address your cover letter to "Dear Pixar Hiring Team" or "Dear Hiring Manager." While we appreciate the personal touch of addressing specific individuals, our team-based approach means multiple people will review your application. Focus on tailoring your content to the specific role rather than finding a particular person's name.

During the application process, you'll find an "Upload Cover Letter" section where you can attach your PDF file. Some positions may also allow you to paste your cover letter text directly into a text field. We recommend having both a PDF version and a text version ready for flexibility.

Keep your demo reel concise and impactful - typically 2-3 minutes maximum. Lead with your strongest work in the first 30 seconds, as this is often when we form our initial impression. Quality over quantity is key; we'd rather see fewer pieces that showcase your best abilities than a longer reel with inconsistent quality.

After submitting your application, you'll receive an email confirmation. Our team typically reviews applications within 2-3 weeks. If we'd like to move forward, we'll contact you to schedule an initial interview. Due to the high volume of applications, we may not be able to provide individual feedback on every submission, but we truly appreciate your interest in joining Pixar.

You can upload your portfolio files directly through our application system or provide links to your online portfolio (website, Vimeo, YouTube, etc.). Make sure any external links are publicly accessible and clearly organized. Include a brief description of each piece and your specific contribution if it was collaborative work.

We recommend applying when you find a role that genuinely matches your skills and interests, rather than applying frequently to multiple positions. If you're not selected for a particular role, we encourage you to continue developing your skills and apply again in the future when new opportunities arise that align with your growth.

While we'd love to provide individual feedback on every application, the volume we receive makes this challenging. However, we occasionally host portfolio review sessions and workshops. Follow our social media channels and careers page for announcements about these opportunities to get valuable feedback from our team.

Most of our positions require authorization to work in the United States. However, we do consider exceptional candidates for roles where we can provide visa sponsorship. Please indicate your visa status clearly in your application. International applicants should also consider our internship programs, which may offer different visa pathways.

We appreciate your creative enthusiasm! However, for legal reasons, we cannot accept unsolicited story or script submissions from external sources. Our stories are developed internally by our creative teams. If you're interested in storytelling at Pixar, consider applying for roles in our Story or Creative Development departments.

Explore our behind-the-scenes content, documentaries, and interviews available on various platforms. We also regularly publish articles about our techniques and philosophy. Additionally, our team members often speak at industry conferences and universities. Following our official channels will keep you updated on these learning opportunities.

Check out our technical papers and presentations at SIGGRAPH and other industry conferences. We also publish research on our website and collaborate with universities on cutting-edge technology. Our RenderMan software documentation and tutorials are excellent resources for understanding our rendering pipeline and techniques.
